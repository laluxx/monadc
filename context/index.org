** LLM Notation
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: monadc.context.format.llm-notation
:CUSTOM_ID: llm-notation
:CONTEXT_KIND: format
:CONTEXT_VERSION: 1
:CONTEXT_CREATED: 2026-06-11
:CONTEXT_DESCRIPTION: LLM-readable context summary
:CONTEXT_UPDATED: 2026-06-11
:SOURCE: context/index.org
:CONFIDENCE: high
:END:

LLM-oriented statements use bracketed records with fixed keys:

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
  [DOC id:<id> src:<file:line> conf:<high|medium|low>] <documentation, bibliographic reference>
  [OBS id:<id> src:<file:line> conf:<high|medium|low>] <direct observation>
[INF id:inf.context.index.record-format-example from:obs.context.index.file conf:high] <inference>
  [DEC id:<id> date:<YYYY-MM-DD> by:<human|codex>] <decision>
  [TODO id:<id> owner:<human|codex|unknown> status:open>] <task>
  [THINK id:<id> conf:<high|medium|low>] <speculative reasoning, exploration note, chain-of-thought>
  [IDEA id:<id> author:<human|llm|human+llm> date:<YYYY-MM-DD>] <design idea, language proposal, feature concept>
#+END_EXAMPLE

Rules:

- =DOC= records documentation, bibliographic references, and papers. They are rendered with richer body text in the dashboard inspector and linked into the graph via source/evidence relations.
- =OBS= is reserved for facts read from files or command output.
- =INF= must name the observation IDs it depends on.
- =DEC= records repository-local choices that future work should preserve.
- =TODO= records pending work only when it is specific and actionable.
- =THINK= records speculative reasoning, exploration notes, or chain-of-thought that is neither a certain observation nor a formal inference nor a binding decision. THINK records are visually distinguished in the dashboard (purple) and filtered out from decisions and formal inferences.
- =IDEA= records a concrete design idea or language feature proposal that is not yet decided or committed. Ideas have an author (the proponent) and a natural lifecycle: IDEA → THINK (analysis) → DEC (accept/reject) or IDEA → TODO (commit to implement) → DONE. Unlike THINK (open speculation), an IDEA is a specific, well-formed proposal. Unlike TODO, it carries no implementation commitment. Multiple IDEA records may compete for the same design space; they are resolved by DEC records.
- IDs are immutable once published.
- Prefer one claim per record.
- Do not silently rewrite history; append a superseding record when facts change.
